## Verdict: build it — but not the way it was first framed

The product is validated: the technical gap is real, the regulatory deadline is weeks away, and the zero-budget MVP is confirmed feasible. The research forced three corrections:

1. **Don't pitch "AI × Blockchain."** Crypto VCs in 2026 explicitly flag that intersection as hype-over-execution (The Block VC surveys, Jan + Apr 2026; ~85% of 2025 VC-backed token launches trade below first valuation). Same product, different words: *content provenance infrastructure with SaaS revenue*. Blockchain is invisible plumbing, not the headline.
2. **Token: never, not just "not now."** All three token precedents in this space are wreckage (§5).
3. **The paying customer is a compliance buyer, not a creator.** Adobe gives creators free signing. The wedge is small generative-AI companies facing EU fines, then e-commerce sellers, then Turkish/MENA legal evidence.

## §1 Why now — a regulatory cascade starting in weeks

EU AI Act Article 50 and California SB 942 take effect **the same day**. The EU's final Code of Practice (published June 10, 2026) describes compliant marking as three layered mechanisms: digitally-signed metadata, invisible watermarking, and fingerprint registries. **That is a description of OzDNA's architecture** — the EU wrote our product category into its own compliance guidance.

| Date | Event | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Jun 10, 2026 | Final EU Code of Practice on marking AI content published | Voluntary. Verified: C2PA is NOT mandated by name — never overclaim |

| **Aug 2, 2026** | EU AI Act Art. 50 applies (fines to €15M / 3% turnover; SMEs pay the lower) + California SB 942 (platforms must **retain** provenance) | The PR moment — one news cycle, zero ad spend |

| Feb 2, 2027 | Interoperable detection solutions required; one sanctioned route is a shared consortium explicitly open to SMEs | Future coalition play, built for a PR founder |

**Turkey angle:** pending parliamentary bill (Nov 2025) mandates "Generated by AI" labels, BTK fines ₺500K–5M; no Turkish-language provenance vendor exists. UAE has no conflicting regime — the Sharjah entity is a clean home.

**Pitch-deck fraud anchors (directional — vendor-aggregated trackers, not independently verified; use as illustration, not proof):** Arup lost $25M to a single deepfake-CFO video call; ~$1.65B global deepfake fraud losses in 2025; 99% of insurers have seen AI-altered claim documentation (Verisk, Mar 2026); Deloitte projects $40B GenAI-enabled fraud in the US by 2027. | **Token graveyard** (NUM, Story/IP, WordProof) | NUM −99.9%, ATL Jun 27, 2026 · Story −98%, rebranded to DATA Foundation Jun 25, 2026 · WordProof token silently dropped, company dormant (Yoast integration deprecated Jul 2024) | 3-for-3: tokens destroyed credibility without funding the business |
| **TrueMedia** (detection nonprofit) | Shut down Jan 2025 — detection compute unsustainable even with philanthropy; now stewarded by Georgetown (Apr 2025) | Keep AI detection out of v1 entirely |

**The open lane (verified):** No shared cross-industry manifest repository exists. Social platforms (Instagram, X, TikTok, Facebook, WhatsApp) still strip C2PA metadata on upload — while LinkedIn (CR badge), YouTube ("captured with a camera"), and Google (About this image; I/O 2026: C2PA verification coming to Search/Chrome) increasingly *display* credentials. The C2PA spec (v2.4, Apr 2026) formally anticipates third-party manifest repositories and publishes a Soft Binding API to build against. The only live implementation is Adobe's — vendor-run, images-only. A **neutral, API-first, multi-format DNA registry** is an unoccupied position; RAND's 2025 analysis of the stripping problem is the independent evidence for why it must exist. Consider implementing TrustMark (open-source, C2PA-approved watermark) alongside perceptual hashing so OzDNA speaks the standard's own soft-binding language.

## §3 Product — sign, anchor, remember

1. **Sign** — C2PA signing in the browser via `@contentauth/c2pa-web` (WASM): the client pays the compute, we pay nothing. Server alternative: `c2pa-node-v2` on Vercel functions (250MB bundle limit is fine; 10s Hobby duration is fine for images). `@trustnxt/c2pa-ts` (pure TS, zero native deps) fits Cloudflare Workers for verification/read paths. Images only in v1.
2. **Anchor** — content hashes batched on-chain for cents (benchmark $0.0001/asset). Chain-agnostic by design → apply to every ecosystem's grants without re-architecture. This is the entire "blockchain" story: neutral, boring, legal everywhere. No custody, no CASP exposure.
3. **Remember (the moat)** — perceptual fingerprint stored at signing so a stripped/re-encoded/screenshotted copy still matches back to its origin record. Platforms can't strip it, and it maps 1:1 onto the EU Code's "fingerprinting backed by registry databases" mechanism.

Out of v1: detection classifiers, video signing, mobile capture apps. **Key management is first-class from day one** — Nikon revoked its entire camera-signing program over one vulnerability (that incident is a pitch slide).

### The one unknown that matters (trust chain)

Self-signed certs validate cryptographically but show **"unknown source"** in the official Verify tool. Trusted status requires the C2PA Conformance Program — fees unpublished, only contact conformance@c2pa.org — and the interim trust list froze Jan 1, 2026 (no shortcut). SSL.com's free cert tier (June 2026: one Level 1 claim-signing cert + 10k timestamps/yr) requires a conformance record ID.
**Posture:** v1 ships its own verify page + blockchain anchor (needs no one's trust list) and never promises "trusted Content Credentials." If conformance costs thousands, it becomes the first grant-funded milestone, not a launch blocker.

## §5 Token & whitepaper — whitepaper yes, token never

- **Numbers Protocol (NUM)** — closest comparable, with real adoption (1.5M app downloads, listed in NIST AI 100-4 appendix, Rolling Stone project) — hit its all-time low June 27, 2026, −99.9% from peak. Maximum thematic validation, total token value destruction. Company survives on grants and pivots.
- **Story Protocol** — $140M raised, a16z-led, an entire L1 for IP provenance — token −98%, first unlock delayed, and on June 25, 2026 rebranded to the DATA Foundation, abandoning the thesis in 16 months.
- **WordProof** — won the EU's €1M blockchain prize, attached a token anyway, token silently abandoned, company now dormant (its Yoast distribution channel deprecated July 2024). Cautionary tale: grants without distribution ≠ survival.
- **VC consensus 2026** (The Block, Jan 4 + Apr 19, 2026): token model "broken down as a reliable exit"; pre-revenue token launches not coming back; crypto-AI flagged by name as hype over execution.

The whitepaper gets written — technical vision document and backbone of every grant application. It states plainly: **token-optional architecture, no token planned.** If the registry ever becomes a decentralized attestation network, that's a legitimate future case — decided later, through a proper legal wrapper, funded by capital that exists by then. Legal note: token issuance from Turkey triggers CASP/securities exposure; the Sharjah SPC entity cannot issue either (not a VASP regime); marketing into the EU triggers MiCA. In 2026, "no token, real revenue" *is* the differentiated position.

## §7 Honest risks

1. **Demand gap.** The legal duty falls on GenAI providers, deepfake deployers, and platforms — not everyday creators — and there is no procurement evidence yet of small companies buying third-party provenance tooling for AI Act compliance. Dec 2 should force it; **validate with the waitlist before building.**
2. **Consolidation risk.** Marking may be absorbed at model-provider/platform level (OpenAI + Google announced a joint dual-layer C2PA+SynthID system on May 19, 2026 — rolling out, not yet fully live). The registry — matching stripped copies back to origin — is the piece their signing doesn't solve; it must stay the core.
3. **Adobe expands.** Free app is images-only beta today; video/audio "coming soon." Speed matters; neutrality (non-Adobe, API-first, multi-format) is the durable differentiation.
4. **Conformance cost unknown.** See §3 posture.
5. **Founder bandwidth.** Solo, MetalTakip 90-day plan mid-flight → two-speed plan below.
