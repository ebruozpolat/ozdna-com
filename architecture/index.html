OZDNA.COM — ARCHITECTURE

How a claim
becomes checkable.

Both products share one job: attach the evidence to the output. For OriginDNA that means giving an image a verifiable origin; for ComplyDNA it means tying every sentence to the statute behind it. This is design-stage — OriginDNA is pre-build ahead of an October 2026 MVP.

[01]ORIGINDNA — THE IMAGE DNA

Three layers,
one origin.

v1 covers images only (JPG/PNG). Each image gets a "DNA" made of three independent layers, so that if one is stripped the others still stand.

01 / SIGN

C2PA signature

A cryptographic Content Credential is attached at creation. In the free flow this is signed in your browser via WebAssembly, so the image never leaves your device to be signed. Paid API tenants can sign server-side at scale.

02 / ANCHOR

Chain timestamp

A hash of the image is anchored to a public blockchain, giving a timestamp no one can backdate. Anchoring is batched and paid by us — ozDNA never holds funds, moves user money, or custodies a wallet.

03 / FINGERPRINT

Perceptual hash

A perceptual fingerprint is recorded so the image stays matchable even after a social platform strips its metadata or re-encodes it — the failure mode that breaks metadata-only approaches.

VERIFY

Verification runs against our own verify page and the chain anchor — not a third party's trust list. Anyone can re-check the signature and the timestamp independently.

Honest status. C2PA is not mandated by name in the EU's rules, and until ozDNA passes the C2PA Conformance Program our credentials will read as "unknown source" in the official Verify tool. v1 ships its own verification and chain anchor and does not promise "trusted Content Credentials." This is provenance — not AI detection; there are no detection classifiers in v1.
[02]COMPLYDNA — THE CITED ANSWER

Every sentence,
traceable to a statute.

  • Grounded retrievalAnswers are drawn from a curated corpus of Turkish regulation — MASAK communiqués, AML/CFT laws 5549 & 6415, KVKK — not open-web guesses.
  • Inline citationsEach claim carries a [TEBLİĞ / Madde] reference, so a compliance officer can jump straight to the article and audit the answer.
  • Deploy your wayCloud API or fully on-premises, so regulated institutions can keep data inside their own boundary.
  • Measured, not assumedAccuracy is measured on each release rather than claimed once — the same discipline as public verification on the OriginDNA side.
[03]SHARED PRINCIPLES

Invisible plumbing. The blockchain anchor is infrastructure, not a pitch — there is no token, and none is planned. The architecture is token-optional by design.

Client pays the compute it can. Free users sign in-browser; paid tenants pay for metered server-side compute. Cost scales with who benefits.

Verification over trust-us. Whether it's a chain anchor or a statutory citation, the output carries what you need to check it yourself.
