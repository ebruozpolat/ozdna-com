# OriginDNA — Proof of Origin

OriginDNA is **content provenance infrastructure**. Every image gets a DNA:

1. **Signature** — a cryptographic signature attached at creation, following the open
   C2PA standard: who made it, with what tool, when. Tampering breaks the signature.
2. **Timestamp** — the image's fingerprint is anchored into a public, append-only record;
   anyone can independently confirm "this existed by this date" without an OriginDNA
   account.
3. **Fingerprint** — a perceptual fingerprint stored at registration, so stripped,
   resized or recompressed copies can be matched back to the registered original.

## Why now — the regulatory clock

- **Aug 2, 2026** — EU AI Act Article 50 applies; California SB 942 takes effect the same day.
  Generative-AI providers must mark AI-generated content in machine-readable form.
- **Dec 2, 2026** — grace period ends for GenAI systems already on the market.
- **Feb 2, 2027** — interoperable detection/marking requirements tighten.

## Who it's for

1. GenAI apps needing EU AI Act marking — self-serve API (primary)
2. Marketplace sellers & creators (Etsy requires AI disclosure since Jan 2026) — badge plans
3. Newsrooms & fact-checking organizations — free, forever
4. Legal / enterprise evidence capture (TR/MENA) — later

## Honest status

- Pre-launch. MVP ships October 2026; launch targets the Dec 2, 2026 compliance wave.
  Waitlist: https://ozdna.com/products/origin/#waitlist
- v1 = images only (JPG/PNG). Video/audio later.
- OriginDNA signs to the open C2PA standard but is **not yet** a certified member of the
  C2PA conformance program and does not claim "trusted Content Credentials" status.
  Verification runs through OriginDNA's own public verify page and public record.
- No token exists or is planned. No user funds are ever held. This is SaaS.
- OriginDNA is provenance, **not** AI detection — it does not classify content as
  AI-generated after the fact.
